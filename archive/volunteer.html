EDDIE SOUZA COMMUNITY GARDEN

12/23/2019 - 2 HRS

The community garden is located on the corner of Monroe and Stevens Creek, and is a beautiful place and a smart idea. I helped with the composting for the winter, by shovelling/raking dirt and leaves, moving tomato vines, and hosing it all down. There were so many green tomatoes on the vines, and most people would toss green tomatoes, including the garden. There was another Indian lady and her kids there as well, and she was collecting the tomatoes. There is a certain chutney you can make from them, and I ended up taking some home as well. The garden is in a really neat location as well, and I can bike there from my house through the San Thomas Aquino bike trail. I would definatly reccommend volunteering there, although it is a bit of work and you'll be covered in dirt, but I enjoyed it!

VSNA REDCROSS BLOOD DRIVE

12/14/2019 - 28 HRS

I first met Rekha Shivapa at the kitchen during a VSNA event. Here's some background on that. VSNA (I forget what it stands for) is an community organization connecting people in the Bay Area from a particular state in India called Karnataka, in which the main language spoken is Kannada. My cousin and I were looking to help out as we were feeling a bit bored, and so ended up stirring a pot in the kitchen. I don't know if you believe in coincidences or fate, but it was fate that ended up with this blood drive happenning. Rekha had run RedCross Drives in the past from VSNA, but due to lack of community support they hadn't done one in a while. She brought the idea up to me and asked if I'd be interested in doing one, and of course the answer was yes! The goal of running this drive was to get the youth support and interest, and through that we made this event happen. It took a few months of planning and finding a place to host it, and eventually someone was able to offer up their neighborhood clubhouse. Without Rekha, this wouldn't have happened at all, and without her experience we would've been so lost. All of us worked together to contact people to be donors, and even my mom donated for her first time and for the hours after she said "I feel so good about it" or something along the lines of that. Some other highschoolers and I worked at the rest station, to make sure that the donors ate and drank something and rested for a 15 minute period before leaving. This experience was new and overall amazing.

FARM CAMP

6/8-9-10-11-12-15-16-17-18/2019 - 46 HRS

Farm Camp was a valuable experience I had this summer. I didn’t know up until this year, but the Full Circle Farm/SCUSD Farm has its own Food and Farm Camp every summer. The campers get to harvest their own fruits and veggies and make their own delicious snack and lunch with them. This was yet another opportunity that Kelsey offered to me, and I also got to meet Mint, who is one of the nicest people ever, and she runs the came every year. Volunteering at this camp made this summer so rewarding. The other volunteers and I helped the counselors keep track of the campers and keep them engaged. We also helped the kids chop up their fruits and veggies and did a lot of the cooking. The first week I volunteered, I was there from 7:30-1:30. The kids were 5-7 years old and loved to help out, though that week we did most of the cooking for them since they were so young. They were all so adorable! We helped they harvest seasonal fruits and veggies, and wash and eat them. They also did crafts and played games which we joined in on. The next week though, I was there from 7:30-3:30. The kids were 8-10 years old and could handle a lot by themselves. I have never met so many kids who liked washing dishes. I enjoyed spending time with the other volunteers and the campers. I met a couple of high school students and two of them were from SCHS, Quinten and Jessica. We talked a lot about K-Pop and music in school, and hobbies/interests. The campers were fun to hang out with and were filled with curiosity and enthusiasm that becomes harder to come by as you grow older! I learned to take initiative and responsibility through these two weeks. I love to cook (but am not great at it) and this gave me the opportunity to improve my skills and get feedback from other too.

WILCOX NEWCOMER PROGRAM

6/19-26-28/2019 - 15 HRS

This volunteer opportunity was unique and a great experience. I volunteered at Wilcox a couple of days to help tutor incoming high school students who came from other countries and did not speak English fluently. I volunteered here in between other things I was doing this summer, for a total of 3 days and 5 hours a day. I found out about this through another student in the SLI program. As a tutor, I helped correct grammar and English and help them out if they were stuck. Throughout the weeks they were working on a Rosetta Stone course and the other tutor and I helped them out if they get stuck on a question. Along with that, the students were working on a WeVideo project and we were able to help out with the video editing and pronunciation in their voice recordings. I enjoyed spending time with a group of people that I wouldn’t normally communicate with because of a language barrier. Many of them had come from Central America and Mexico, but some had come from Vietnam, India, and other countries. It definately would have helped to have some Spanish background, as it was tough to communicate effectively, but after that experience I think I am better at putting complex thoughts in simpler terms, which will help so, so much for tutoring and being able to explain things in the future. I had a great time helping them improve their English and getting to know them.

49ERS SLI TRANSFER APPLICATION INTERVIEWS

1/30/2019 - 3.5 HRS

To be part of 49ers SLI, you have to go through a two-step application process, the second part being the interview. I didn't get selected through the interview for my 7th grade year, but I did for my 8th grade. In the end, I think it worked out better for me,but it would have been nice to be part of the program for all 6 years. Anyway, each year they have transfer applications to fill in spots of students that left the previous year, and there are only 60 spots total per cohort. I particularly felt more connected to the people I met here, because I got to share my experiences and how things had gone for me being in Peterson Middle for two years and transfering into the the second year of my cohort in the program. I really hope that in the future I get to meet some of the people I talked to. Personally, I feel like the students that get in through tranferring are really the nicest people from what I've seen. They persevere and show passion for what they do, especially if they didn't get in in their first year when they were competing for a spot with 300 other kids.
